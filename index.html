Why Islam?

Islam is the true religion because it as the final and complete revelation of God's message, delivered through Prophet Muhammad and preserved in the Quran. The Quran contains verbatim words of God, supported by scientific facts, and that the Prophet's example, the Sunnah, provides a comprehensive guide for life. The faith's emphasis on the oneness of God (tawhid), the promise of an afterlife, and the performance of the Five Pillars of Islam are central to the fact of being the path to spiritual peace and purpose.

The Five Pillars of Islam

Shahada (Faith)

The declaration of faith in one God (Allah) and His messenger (peace be upon him).

Salah (Prayer)

The ritual prayer required of every Muslim five times a day throughout their lifetime.

Zakat (Almsgiving)

The act of giving a portion of a Muslim’s wealth to those in need throughout their lifetime.

Sawm (Fasting)

The act of fasting during the holy month of Ramadan.

Hajj (Pilgrimage)

The sacred pilgrimage to Mecca required of every Muslim at least once in their lifetime if it is within their means.
